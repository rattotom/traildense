§ 02 — premise

Roads flatten the world into routes.
Trails fold it back into terrain.
So we record the folds.

Every commercial mapping platform we tried treated singletrack as an afterthought — a thin grey line laid over a road network. We wanted the opposite: a system that starts from the trail, weights it by the bodies that have moved across it, and lets the topology emerge from the ride data itself.

Traildense records GPS at 2 s / 5 m intervals through a foreground service, filters out paved overlaps with OSM road buffers, and aggregates the survivors into a density tile. The more a line is ridden, the brighter it gets. The forgotten ones fade.

§ 03 — density

A heatmap of belonging.

Each anonymized track is buffered, road-filtered, then accumulated onto a vector tile pyramid. Line weight, opacity and colour are all functions of n — the number of distinct riders on the segment.

§ 04 — field kit

What ships in week one.

01recording

Foreground service that doesn't quit.

FusedLocationProvider at 2 s / 5 m, GPS-jump filter, START · PAUSE · RESUME · STOP intents — keeps recording even when the OS would rather sleep.

service/TrackingService.kt
02storage

Room database, foreign keys, cascade delete.

Rides and TrackPoints persist locally first. Sync is opt-in. The phone is the source of truth until you say otherwise.

data/db/AppDatabase.kt
03map

MapLibre vector tiles, no API key.

OpenFreeMap Liberty style by default, lifecycle-aware, follows the rider position. Topo, hillshade and contour layers ship in week three.

ui/component/MapLibreView.kt
04HUD

Glanceable. Distraction-free.

Distance · time · elevation gain · speed (live / avg / max). One-handed pause and stop. Optional: hide it all and just ride.

ui/component/RideHud.kt
05filter

OSM road overlap, removed server-side.

Spatial buffering accounts for GPS drift. Fire roads and mixed-surface connectors get flagged, not deleted — they earn their own styling.

pipeline/road-filter
06offline

MBTiles per region. Δ-sync only.

Background updates fetch only changed tiles, throttled to charging + Wi-Fi + idle. A storage dashboard shows every byte you've cached.

tiles/region-cache
§ 05 — instrument

The HUD is the product.

Five numbers, an aerial map, two thumbs of control. Everything else lives one swipe away. The post-ride screen asks one question: upload to the public density map? Yes opens the trail to other riders. No keeps the line yours.
